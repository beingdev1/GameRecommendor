We're about to see OG back at a Major tournament with their star carry Anathan 'ana' Pham in Disneyland. However, first we look back to a time when the reigning TI8 champion won their last Major.

Posted by Jack in General |

The favourites were completely different back then: while we consider OG's TI victory one of the biggest upsets in Dota 2 history, nobody would be surprised if OG would have won The International 2017. Johan 'BigDaddyN0tail' Sundstein and his team-mates were the best team in the world. ana and OG setting records Since the Major circuit started in the end of 2015, only two different teams won a Major. OG won four of them - and the last one exactly one year ago. The lineup of OG included three current TI winners back then: N0tail, ana and Jesse 'JerAx' Vainikka. Offlaner Sebastien 'Ceb' Debs was part of the lineup as a coach of the squad. The other two players were Gustav 's4' Magnusson and Tal 'Fly' Aizik. This team had dominated the Dota 2 scene for the last couple of months before Kiev Major happened and of course, they were the favourites to win the trophy.

Virtus.pro's first Major success They were in good shape during the group stage and lost only one game to Invictus Gaming with the star players Xu 'BurNIng' Zhilei and Ye 'BoBoKa' Zhibiao. Both top teams in the group stage were Team Secret and Virtus.pro were the two top teams in the group stage, with Ilya 'Lil' Ilyuk playing for the Russian outfit back then. It was the first time they had a top-notch performance at a Major event - nobody thought that this could be OG's last Major success, while Virtus.pro's era would start right there.
